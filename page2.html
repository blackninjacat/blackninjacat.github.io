Oxidative Phosphorylation

This process takes place in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Reduced NAD and Reduced FAD are passed to the electron transprt chain. Hydrogen atoms are removed from thir respective carriers and are then split into protons and electrons. the energetic electron is then transferred to the first in a series of electron carrier molecules in the electron transport chain. Electrons travel down an energy gradient from one carrier molecule to another. This provides energy for the proton pump proteins to pump the protons from the mitochondrial matrix into the intermembrane space. The protons then diffuse down its concentration gradient through a proton channel protein associated with ATP Synthase. The pH gradient represents electrical potential energy. As the protons diffuse through the proton channel, electrical potential energy is used to synthesise ATP from ADP and inorganic phosphate in a reaction catalysed by ATP Synthase. The protons, now in the mitochondrial matrix then react with oxygen and the electrons from whch they were originally split from, water(H2O) is produced. Oxidised NAD and oxidised FAD are also regenerated at the end of oxidative phosphorylation
